# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the SmartStock project and its communities will be treated with respect and dignity.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Documenting decisions with clear reasoning
- Respecting architectural boundaries and ADRs
- Providing thorough code reviews that help improve code quality

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment of any kind
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Violating confidentiality agreements
- Code that intentionally circumvents architectural decisions
- Committing secrets or sensitive data to the repository
- Ignoring security vulnerabilities or bypassing security checks
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate corrective action in response to instances of unacceptable behavior.

Community members who do not follow the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by the project leadership.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community.

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior — or have any other concerns — please report it by emailing the project maintainers. All communications will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially.

Those who are asked to stop unacceptable behavior are expected to comply immediately.
